# Aevum Trademark Policy

## Overview

"Aevum" and the Aevum logo are trademarks of Aevum Labs contributors.
This policy describes what you may and may not do with these marks.

## Permitted Uses

You may use the Aevum name to:

- Accurately refer to the Aevum software project or its components.
- Indicate that your product is compatible with, or built on top of, Aevum.
- Describe Aevum in educational, journalistic, or commentary contexts.
- Distribute unmodified copies of the Aevum software under the Apache-2.0 license.

When referring to your Aevum-based product, use language such as:
> "Built with Aevum" or "Powered by Aevum"

## Restrictions

You may **not**:

- Use "Aevum" in the name of your company, product, or service in a way that implies official endorsement or affiliation.
- Register "Aevum" or confusingly similar marks as trademarks.
- Use the Aevum logo without prior written permission.
- Modify the Aevum logo and claim the result is the official Aevum logo.
- Use the marks in any way that suggests your offering is the official Aevum project.

## Community Forks and Derivatives

If you fork Aevum and distribute a modified version, you must:

1. Rename your distribution so it is clearly distinguishable from the official project.
2. Remove all official Aevum logos and branding from your distribution.
3. Include a clear statement that your distribution is a fork and is not the official Aevum project.

## Questions

For trademark questions or permission requests, open an issue at
<https://github.com/aevum-labs/aevum/issues> or contact the maintainers
listed in GOVERNANCE.md.
